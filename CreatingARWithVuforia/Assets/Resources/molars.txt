Molars

Artifact Analysis
A common cause of death for hippos is the overgrowth of the molars hampering the ability to grind down food and eat. Older hippos on the refuge are given a special operation to help reduce molar size.

Fact
Hippos are mostly herbivorous the molars serve to grind down plant material for digestion. Meat is only eaten during times of extreme duress. Adult hippos have approximately 36 teeth counting the incisors, canines, and premolars. 

Zuri: / Okonkwo:
Barbell carp are known for eating the material in the hippos teeth acting acting as a dentist of sorts. Okonkwo is known for spending large amounts of time in the water.
